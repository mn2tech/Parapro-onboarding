Welcome to the Student Safety Response Protocol training module. This training provides essential guidance for educational support staff working in school settings.

In school environments, various situations may arise that require immediate attention and appropriate response. These situations can present in different ways. You may notice sudden changes in student behavior or responsiveness. You might observe unusual movements or physical responses. There could be changes in student awareness or consciousness. Physical distress signals may be present. Unexpected physical reactions can occur. It is important to be observant and recognize when a student may need immediate support.

When responding to any situation that requires immediate attention, follow these important response steps.

Step One: Maintain Your Composure. Take a moment to breathe and remain calm. Your composed demeanor helps create a safe environment for everyone. Clear thinking is essential during these situations.

Step Two: Create a Safe Environment. Remove any nearby objects that could cause harm. Ensure the area around the student is clear. Provide cushioning if possible and safe to do so. Never attempt to restrain a student during these situations. Never place objects in a student's mouth.

Step Three: Monitor the Timing. Note the exact time when the situation began. Most situations resolve within a few minutes. If a situation continues beyond five minutes, contact support services immediately.

Step Four: Positioning for Safety. If safe to do so, gently help position the student on their side. This positioning can help maintain clear airways. Adjust any clothing that may restrict normal breathing.

Step Five: Observation and Documentation. Monitor the student's condition carefully. Note the duration of the situation. Observe and remember specific behaviors or responses for documentation.

Contact support services immediately if the situation continues for more than five minutes. Contact support if a second situation begins before the student fully recovers. Contact support if the student sustains any harm during the situation. Contact support if there are concerns about the student's normal breathing. Contact support if the situation occurs in a potentially dangerous environment like water. Contact support if the student has documented health conditions that require immediate professional attention.

After the situation has resolved, remain with the student until they are fully alert and responsive. Use calm, reassuring communication. Understand that confusion or fatigue afterward is common. Allow adequate time for recovery. Document the incident following your school's established procedures. Communicate with parents or guardians as required by your school's policy.

Remember, your role is to maintain student safety and provide supportive care. Stay calm, follow your school's established protocols, and contact support services when necessary. Thank you for completing this important training module.
